Nagas are reptilian people with snake-like lower bodies and lizard-like upper bodies. They have unhingeable jaws like snakes, but chameleon-like tongues used for quickly catching smaller prey. They are typically found in desert and jungle areas, anywhere significantly hot to benefit their cold-blooded metabolism. Their eyes have secondary transparent eyelids that lets them easily navigate sandstorms.

Before the Biometal War, nagas also participated in hunting kholu and marstels, using their common affinity for shock magic to stun them from a distance. In the current era, they no longer actively hunt others, but still practice slavery, and will sometimes use coiling or devouring as a punishment.

Their culture is extremely conservative. Their religion teaches that they are the enlightened people, leading to them seeing others as lesser than themselves, which is why they never gave up such practices. Nagas worship Jameki, a six-winged naga known for being a trickster god who used his cunning to overthrow the rest of the Kyrodian pantheon and establish his chosen people as the rightful rulers of the world. While the biometal war necessitated giving up their conquering ways for survival, other races living in their borders are treated as second-class for being gentiles to their religion.

One cultural teaching is the surpression of emotional response, basically training a poker face, to make it harder for others to read them. This makes it easier to disarm others who might try posturing to intimidate them, expose weakness during debate or negotiation, and look down on those with less emotional control.

Being connected to the shock element, their main artistic product is fulgur glass. Using lightning to strike sand, they can create glass far faster and more easily than others, mixing in other minerals for color, and then filing the glass down smooth. They can even used sustained lightning to keep the glass hot for shaping.
