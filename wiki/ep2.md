# Blood Victual

## Playable Characters

* [Trey Nuberro](trey.md)
* [Sophie Nuberro](sophie.md)

## Story

Trey and Sophie are summoned to a mysterious castle by a letter claiming they are needed to discuss an inheritance. When they arrive, they are greeted by the castle butler, Haskell, and introduced to the other "candidates". Once everyone is gathered, they are introduced to Count Lupin, a [marstel](marstels.md) vampire who owns the castle.

Lupin tells them that only one may inherit his gift: becoming a full-blooded vampire. He reveals that everyone gathered here is half-vampire. He also tells them that none may leave until a winner is found. They are each sent to their own rooms to prepare, and then must seek each other out and duel for each other's powers.

Trey and Sophie are separated at first, and after finding each other, team back up. After defeating the others, they go to Lupin's tower to confront him. He tells them they must now fight each other or both shall perish here. Naturally, they stick together and defeat Lupin.

Haskell comes and tells them they have earned the right to rule the castle. They tell him they have no interest in ruling over an army of undead, command them all to rest, and leave the castle, choosing instead to take Lupin's airship for themselves. Haskell thanks them for being allowed to rest before fading away.

## Candidates

* Marie Annette - A [kholu](kholu.md) with the power of compulsion, with a bodyguard thrall. After her thrall is defeated (rather easily), she flees through a window and gets immediately devoured by a giant plant, which is the real boss. Haskell clarifies that her ability will not be granted since she took her own life, but as a consolation, Sophie is granted a seed sniper that can bind enemies in vines.

Marie sees the selection rite as a form of refinement. Her power to control others has given her a sense of superiority that's very uncommon among kholu. She's become dependent on this ability to the point where it's affected her philosophy; she needs to control to be in control, and when her thrall is defeated, her loss of control is what triggers her to flee.

What Trey and Sophie take from her is that obsessive control leads to overdependence. Marie depended on power that prevented feedback, and in doing so never learned how to stand on her own when her support structure was taken away, an ironic lesson given that's a central teaching among kholu. It strengthens Trey and Sophie's resolve to see this ordeal through together.

* Dexter Quailheart - A [hidegvin](hidegvins.md) with the power of bat form. Tries to flee, but is devoured by the next candidate in the process.

Dexter has always seen his ability as an opportunity, but he didn't know exactly what. He came curious about whether or not Lupin's inheritance would shed light on his powers. Before coming, transformation was a convenience, and once he learns what the rite means, he sees it as his only means of survival. He is repeatedly encountered by Trey and Sophie, who try to offer him partnership, but he can't trust them, and that leads him straight into the jaws of Flint.

His death teaches Trey and Sophie that the cost of agency is the freedom to make catastrophically wrong choices. Dexter rejected the game as Trey and Sophie do, but let his fear control him.

* Flint Mistwing - A [gryphon](gryphons.md) with the power of blood drain. Devours Dexter when he tries to flee. When defeated, he imparts both abilities, granting Trey and Sophie both the bat form transformation and life steal for Trey's melee attacks. His battle involves using the gryphon primal transformation. Technically imparts the ability to drink blood, but they choose not to use that.

Flint starts out seeing the rite as an opportunity to simply increase his power and station. By the time he encounters Trey and Sophie again, he's completely given into it. He shows no remorse about killing Dexter, loving the new power, even appearing more animalistic.

* Asher Jablonski - A [naga](nagas.md) with the power of mist form. He mixes this with his species connection to shock magic, which allows a desperation attack where he turns into a storm cloud, forcing the twins to find cover and dodge sparks until his power runs out.

Asher sees his power as a test from Jameki, that the demi vampirism was allowed into him at conception to test his resolve. Every time he uses it, the feeling of his body turning to mist, he feels like he might lose himself. He's always had to pull himself back together each time, and wonders if the rite is a reflection of his lived experience at a greater scale. If he accepts the dark gift, he risks losing himself at a spiritual level just as turning to mist risks losing his form.

He doesn't express anger when the twins beat him. Instead he tells them that he believes he passed the test, and now it's their turn to take it. What they take from him is that he was alone, while Trey and Sophie have each other. If either of them starts to slip, they'll pull each other back.

* Haskell - A [marstel](marstels.md) with the power to pass through mirrors into an alternate dimension. He is not a boss, nor is his power gained from one.

Haskell is presented as Lupin's butler and guides the candidates, keeping them reminded of the rules of the rite. When Trey and Sophie are all that remain, he tells them that per the rules, they must now face each other; only one may reach Lupin and take his dark gift. When they refuse, that's when he reveals that he was a candidate himself who made the same choice, but failed to defeat Lupin, and was enslaved for it.

He gives his power up willingly, and tells them, "If you win, don't let this continue."

Through the mirror dimension, Trey and Sophie fight their reflections, not just equals in power, but also versions of themselves that accept their place and embrace vampirism. When they defeat them, they're left with a moment, a calm before the storm, to consider these images of what they could have become had they chosen differently, and what they must not let themselves become in the future.

## Count Lupin

The final boss. On the way to Lupin's chamber, the twins find a room where the souls of past candidates are trapped, each one adding to the castle's power. It shows them that it's not just the rite of selection that Haskell wants them to end, but the enslavement of those who failed to meet Lupin's standards. It's also what will become of them if they fail.

When they reach Lupin, he comments on how they should not both be here, and calls Haskell a traitor who has forgotten his place. "Your deaths will serve as a reminder to him."

From the very beginning of the game, Lupin presents himself as the ultimate certainty, demonstrating his belief that he is right and everyone else will come to see it. He lets pushback from others wash over him like water, even viewing setbacks and betrayal as something that will eventually be corrected. Immortality has made him patient, which makes him hard to trip up. It takes him underestimating the power of familial bond to finally bring him down.
