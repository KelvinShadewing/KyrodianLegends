# Zero

**FULL NAME:** Zero  
**RACE:** [Biometal](biometals.md)  
**SEX:** Null  
**AGE:** Unknown  
**HEIGHT:** 4'2"  
**WEIGHT:** ...  
**COLORS:** Dark green hull, violet eyes  
**FACTION:** [Cold Front](coldfront.md), formerly Apex Technologies

Zero is the original mind to occupy [Hunter's](hunter.md) body. After the lab was destroyed by a biometal incursion, Zero was left in his pod, unable to escape. He remained conscious, and learned to turn his telepathy into technopathy, eventually using it to transfer his mind into the biometal hive that was built around the lab. Finding his way into a hatchery, he hacked it to construct a new body for himself, eventually emerging as a biometal shadewing.

After [Midi](midi) broke into the lab, he and Zero briefly fought. Damaged, Zero feigned his destruction, and was later found and repaired by [Dr. Quincey](quincey.md) when the Cold Front raided Gulsandur after Midi's betrayal.

Zero is fiercely loyal to Quincey, and the Cold Front by extension. Despite his abilities, and the fact that as a biometal overseer, he could have an army of his own, he sees himself as a weapon, and a weapon needs to be used. He is capable of hacking non-sapient biometals to override swarm control, which is used to supplement Cold Front forces.

Refers to Hunter as "the usurper" for having his original body. His personal goal is to return himself to an organic body, since his biometal body lacks the sensory richness of an organic one. What Zero doesn't know is that if he switches bodies back with Hunter, he'll get everything he asked for and nothing that he wanted. As soon as he gains Hunter's emotional framework, he'll be forced to reconcile with everything he's done leading up to his victory all at once.

Aside from his personal desire to have back what he lost, Zero has fully rejected the idea of agency. He was created to be a weapon, so he accepts that as what he should be.

For speech patterns, Zero tends to speak in overly-concise sentences, abandoning any sort of decorative language and even sight words unless neccesary. "Example required. Providing example. Meaning apparent. Satisfactory?"
