## Frequently Asked Questions

**Do I need to include the full license text in my work?**

No, not if it is included somewhere it can be easily found with it, such as a link in a work's description. For printed works such as books, it may be included in the front or back inner page of the book. For other physical merchandise, make sure the KLPL can be accessed from wherever they are sold. For online works, including a link in a description, pinned post, or about page is sufficient.

**Can I post my fanwork on a site that doesn’t support CC-BY-SA or GPL licenses?**

If a platform does not allow compliance with the KLPL (for example, by restricting redistribution or access to source materials), you must provide KLC separately in a manner that is reasonably accessible and usable for downstream recipients. If you distribute KLC through a platform that restricts user rights, you must provide an alternative means that is publicly accessible, reasonably discoverable, and provided without additional restrictions for recipients to access and reuse KLC in compliance with the KLPL.

Material is considered separable where it can reasonably be identified, extracted, or recreated without requiring access to non-KLC content.

**If someone else writes a fanfic using my OC + KLC, do I lose rights to my OC?**

You retain rights to your original contributions. However, if your character is derived from or extends KLC (for example, by using KL species or lore), it may be considered a derivative of KLC to the extent it incorporates protected expressive elements (not general ideas, themes, or tropes).

**Can I release my own characters under the KLPL?**

Yes. You may choose to license your own work under the KLPL, but the license is primarily designed for works involving KLC.

**Can my game use proprietary assets alongside KLC assets?**

Only KLC and derivative elements must be licensed under the KLPL (and the applicable copyleft license specified by the KLPL for software components). Independent assets may remain under their own licenses if they remain separable.

**What happens if I mix KLC with another open license?**

This is allowed if KLC remains separable. Each license applies independently; you may not relicense KLC under a different license or apply other licenses to KLC portions.

**Do I have to credit previous fan contributors?**

If your work derives from theirs, yes. Think of it like a tree. If you start from a branch, credit everyone leading back to the root to the extent such contributors can be reasonably identified based on available information, but if you start directly from the root, you only need to credit Kelvin Shadewing. Attribution helps preserve collaborative culture and respects the chain of derivatives.

**Does changing a KLC character’s appearance require extra attribution?**

Not as long as the work makes it clear who they are.

**How should I credit multiple authors in a collaborative work?**

Simply list them all by name.

**Can I shorten the attribution if it’s too long for a social media post?**

If you must, but a follow-up post would be preferable. Shorten only if necessary, but provide a full attribution somewhere accessible.

**Can someone redistribute my fanfic if I forgot to include proper attribution?**

Yes. In fact, this is why it's important to specify which characters are your own so they are not mistaken for KLC. Corrected versions may be redistributed under the KLPL to restore compliance.

**What should I do if I accidentally mislabel KLC content?**

If physical goods are affected, issue a corrected version as soon as practicable. Earlier versions may remain in circulation but may not be fully compliant until corrected.

**Can I include KLC in a paid product like Patreon or Kickstarter?**

Yes. Be aware that recipients are permitted to redistribute KLC in accordance with the KLPL.

**Should I keep my work on a git repo?**

Not required, but is recommended, since it lets people easily track contributors and project history.

**What if separating KLC with original/proprietary content ruins the story?**

The license does not guarantee narrative integrity, only reuse rights.

**Is the KLPL a copyright license or a contract?**

It's a copyright license that conditions permission on compliance with its terms.

**Does use without signing constitute assent?**

Permission is granted subject to compliance with the license terms; use outside those terms exceeds the scope of the license. This is also done in software licenses such as the GNU General Public License, and in Creative Commons licenses.

**What does ‘substantive part of the work’ mean?**

“Substantive” means that the Kyrodian Legends character, setting, or element materially contributes to the identity, presentation, or user experience of the work, rather than appearing incidentally or decoratively.

**Does a cover image count as use of KLC?**

Yes. Use of KLC in a cover image is use of KLC for purposes of this license. Obligations apply only to the KLC content actually used. However, use of KLC in a cover image without substantive appearances in the work itself is discouraged to avoid potential false advertising liability.

**How does the license apply to crossover works?**

Copyleft obligations apply to KLC and to any portions of the work derived from or inseparable from KLC. Original, independent material may be licensed separately.

**Can I comply by releasing a KLC-only version?**

Yes. Here's a few examples for different use cases. The following are non-exhaustive examples of ways compliance may be achieved. They are illustrative, not mandatory. Compliance methods must not unreasonably shift the burden of extraction or reconstruction onto downstream users.

* **Book:** A digital copy in which original character names are replaced with markers that downstream users may find and replace mad-lib style.

* **3D Movie:** Footage in which KLC appears; cropping is allowed, and/or 3D models and voice clips used to make the characters.

* **2D Animation:** Footage in which KLC appears; cropping is allowed. Best practice would be to put KLC on a separate cell and release them as a chroma mask. In cases of vector animations, release vector puppets instead of, or in addition to, relevant footage.

* **Video Game:** For interactive works, compliance may be achieved by releasing KLC-related code and assets in a reusable form, such as a separate module, branch, or mod.

* **Still images/drawings:** Release individual layers containing KLC. For 3D renders, provide or link to relevant models. If neither is possible, allow the community to crop/trace KLC.

* **Music:** Portions of music deriving from KL's soundtrack, if other parts derive from other sources. Original songs focusing on or made for KL should also be released under the KLPL.

You may always release KLC in isolation or modular form to comply, regardless of other content.

**What happens if compliance is not possible at first?**

Where immediate compliance is impracticable, a reasonable period to cure is permitted, taking into account the nature of the work, distribution method, and feasibility of correction.

**Can third parties preserve KLC if compliance fails?**

Yes. This is a conditional permission granted to the public. Community members may only act to restore compliance, not to take control of or republish entire works beyond KLC components, and only to the minimum extent necessary to achieve that purpose.

**Does depiction of a Kyrodian Legends character in promotional materials count as use of KLC?**

Yes. Promotional use of KLC is permitted only to describe a work that itself incorporates KLC and complies with the KLPL.

Use of KLC in advertising for works that do not include KLC is not permitted under this license. If your project doesn’t include KLC, don’t use KLC to promote it.

**How does KLPL apply to characters that I make?**

KLPL applies to the extent the character is derived from KLC (e.g., they come from Kyroda or are a Kyrodian race/species). You retain attribution rights, but others may use them under the KLPL.

Mere inspiration is not sufficient to bring a work within the scope of the KLPL.

Use of distinctive species traits, named settings, or established lore elements specific to KLC will generally be considered incorporation of protected expression.

**Can someone “be” my character or impersonate me?**

No.

While KL-derived characters may be reused under the terms of the KLPL, this does not grant anyone the right to impersonate a real person, falsely claim to be you, or represent their work as being authored by you.

You retain authorship credit for your original contributions to your character. General laws concerning fraud, misrepresentation, defamation, and personality rights continue to apply independently of the KLPL.

Others may write stories that include or reinterpret KL-derived characters, but they may not claim to be you, claim your authorship, imply your endorsement, or present themselves as your real-world identity.

The KLPL governs creative reuse of fictional elements. It does not override identity, attribution, or anti-impersonation laws.

Kyrodian Legends encourages respectful participation. While the KLPL permits reuse of derivative characters as part of an open mythology, misrepresentation of authorship, impersonation of real individuals, or attempts to create confusion regarding identity or endorsement violate both the Trademark Policy and general legal principles and may result in enforcement action.

**Why do you need a trademark policy?**

Kyrodian Legends is structured as an open mythology without a single narrative canon. Trademark policy exists solely to prevent confusion about endorsement or official status, not to control creative direction.

**What if an artist is commissioned to draw a Kyrodian Legends character but was not aware of the KLPL?**

If an artwork depicts Kyrodian Legends characters, species, or other Kyrodian Legends Content (KLC), the artwork is considered a derivative of KLC under the KLPL.

This means that public distribution of the artwork must comply with the KLPL regardless of whether the artist was personally aware of the license at the time the work was created.

Artists who do not wish their work to be distributed under the KLPL may choose to avoid drawing Kyrodian Legends characters or species. Once KLC is used, the KLPL governs distribution of that work.

Clients commissioning artwork should inform artists when a character belongs to the Kyrodian Legends universe so that artists can make an informed decision before accepting the commission.

**What if a client asks an artist to draw a Kyrodian Legends character but does not disclose that it is KLC?**

Misrepresenting a Kyrodian Legends character or species as an unrelated original character does not change the licensing status of the resulting artwork. If the artwork depicts KLC, the KLPL still governs public distribution.

Clients are expected to communicate honestly with artists about the nature of the character being drawn. Misrepresentation may create a dispute between the client and the artist, but it does not override the KLPL.

**Is there a canon?**

No. This is a mythology, and mythology has no singular canon. The 'official' materials are just a baseline for reference and a starting point for new audiences and creators, a simple place to branch from if needed. You are free to diverge from it in any direction you choose, and not bound to the reference continuity.

If any official media refers to material as 'canon', it only refers to material of official origin or has been adopted into the reference continuity. You are under no obligation to adhere to it.

**Do I need to release source files for physical works?**

If source files were used to produce them, yes. Physical works that never had source files to work from are not required to create new source files solely for compliance, but must provide any source materials that exist or were retained in the normal course of production.

**Can I stop using CC-BY-SA and switch to KLPL?**

Yes. If a KLPL-designated version is available, you may use it under the KLPL going forward. To ensure compliance, obtain the material from an official source clearly labeled as KLPL.

If you already have KLC that is licensed under both CC-BY-SA and KLPL, you must respect both licenses. You may choose which license to operate under, but your choice does not remove the obligations of the other license for prior copies.

Use KLPL copies if available, but never ignore the original license.

**Documentation Metadata**

Creators are encouraged to use a JSON-based metadata system to help keep track of projects. This metadata is designed to support automated tooling, attribution, dependency resolution, and archival systems, and should remain simple, stable, and forward-compatible.

The following is an example of what this data may look like.

```json
{
	"license": "KLPL-1.0",
	"manifest_version": "1.0",
	"id": "from-golden-sands-kshadewing-2026",
	"version": "1.0.0",
	"published": "2026-03-14",
	"name": "From Golden Sands",
	"kind": "campaign",
	"credit": [
		{
			"name": "Kelvin Shadewing",
			"role": ["director", "writer", "artist"]
		}
	],
	"derived_from": ["Kyrodian Legends"],
	"dependencies": [
		{
			"id": "core",
			"version": "1.0.0",
			"required": true
		}
	],
	"description": "The origin story where Midi and Hunter meet.",
	"tags": ["platformer", "adventure", "game"],
	"url": "https://codeberg.org/KelvinShadewing/kyrodian-legends",
	"entries": [
		{
			"path": "maps/world-map.tmj",
			"type": "map",
			"required": true,
			"hash": "6f60c656c910eb2b42047812dc70acf685f84c08d135399f616cd38c6f00aea7"
		}
	]
}
```

`license`: The license version the module was released under.
`manifest_version`: Version of the metadata format.
`id`: A unique ID for use in engines. Should be formatted to help prevent name conflicts. This is useful in game engines, wiki/archival systems, and searchable databases. It's recommended to use creator names, dates, and other info as suffixes to help avoid conflicts.
`version`: Self explanatory. Version numbers should use `major.minor.patch` semantics.
`published`: Publication date of the work (ISO 8601 format recommended: YYYY-MM-DD).
`name`: Name of the work itself.
`kind`: Used to categorize the module for uses similar to `id`. This can be used to define if the work is a story/campaign, character, setting, faction, library, or other assets.
`credit`: Contributor(s) to the module. Can be used to generate in-work credits/staff rolls and organize modules by contributors in wikis.
`derived_from`: For upstream tracking. Useful for attribution, especially for automating it.
`dependencies`: Separate from `derived_from`, intended specifically for modding so game engines know how to order loading and check that needed materials exist. This allows the creation of shared libraries without having to derive from lore. Version number is to make sure installed modules also are up to date. Version matching may be implementation-dependent.
`description`: Self explanatory.
`tags`: Optional data for categorization and search.
`url`: Optinal URL for project source. Useful or end users to find updates or for wikis to link external sources.
`entries`: Optional list of files for automated loading/execution and verifying package contents. Hashes can be included to check for file modification.

It's recommended to store module definitions in a file like `name.klc.json` or in the case of things like Tiled map files, add a root-level field called `klc`.

Note that this is not mandatory, but makes indexing and collaboration much easier, as this data allows automated indexing and management systems.

Here's a format for indexing modules. This can be used to discover, fetch, and manage Kyrodian content across decentralized sources.

```json
{
	"schema": "1.0",
	"updated": "2026-03-17T12:00:00Z",
	"modules": [
		{
			"id": "from-golden-sands-kshadewing-2026",
			"versions": {
				"version": "1.0.0",
				"url": "https://example.com/modules/from-golden-sands.klc.json"
			}
		}
	]
}
```

## Community Curing Guidelines

The KLPL empowers community members to assist in restoring compliance with the license, but such actions are strictly limited in scope and must be exercised responsibly. The following rules define the boundaries of legitimate community-curing:

**Scope and Purpose**

Community-curing is solely intended to restore compliance with the KLPL. It does not authorize extraction, redistribution, or modification of non-KLC content, nor does it grant any rights beyond those explicitly provided by the license.

Actions must be proportional, focused on correcting licensing, attribution, or separability issues, and limited to KLC or derivative works thereof. Actions must not exceed what is strictly necessary to restore compliance.

**Temporal and Situational Limits**

Community-curing may not occur before the work has been publicly Distributed. If the original distributor is actively curing in good faith, community intervention must pause until compliance is complete. Once a work is fully compliant, community-curing rights with respect to that work immediately cease.

**Ethical Conduct**

Community-curing must not be used for harassment, retaliation, or to coerce authors. Members must not misrepresent the original author’s intent, assert ownership, or claim exclusive control over KLC. Activities must avoid unnecessary exposure of proprietary or sensitive materials unrelated to KLC.

**Authority and Liability**

Only the copyright holders of KLC or their authorized agents may formally enforce rights under the KLPL. Community members act voluntarily and are not agents of the copyright holders. Unauthorized, excessive, or abusive actions may create independent legal liability for the actor. The copyright holders disclaim responsibility for community actions beyond the scope of this license.

**Cultural Spirit**

The KLPL promotes openness, collaboration, and continuity of KLC. Members should prioritize dialogue, transparency, and good faith over coercion, intimidation, or reputation attacks. Compliance and community enforcement should advance the shared mythology without creating a policing culture.

## KLPL Icon

This icon is for creators to use to indicate that a work is licensed under the KLPL without embedding the full license text. Use the icon whenever including the full license is impractical, such as in social media posts, small promotional images, merchandise tags, or in-game UI elements.

**Does using the icon replace the license?**

No. The icon does not replace the full license. It is a visual indicator that KLPL applies. Whenever feasible, include a link or reference to the full license text so users can access the complete terms.

**How should I display the icon?**

Place it in a visible location near the work, e.g., in an “About,” “Credits,” or “Licenses” section. If space is limited, a tooltip or hover text linking to the full license is acceptable. Ensure it is clear that the icon represents KLPL licensing, not endorsement or sponsorship by the Kyrodian Legends project or Kelvin Shadewing.

For digital works/webpages, you may embed a link pointing to the full text of the KLPL. Alt text or tooltip should describe the icon and mention the license (e.g., “Kyrodian Legends Public License (KLPL) in effect”). This helps accessibility and makes the link discoverable for screen readers. For static images (like PDFs or screenshots), include the URL nearby in plain text so users can type it manually.
