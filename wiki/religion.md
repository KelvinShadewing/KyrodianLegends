# Religion

## The Cults of Pyrael and Loper

Kyroda's most well-known religion focuses on Pyrael (pronounced peer-ay-ell), the spirit of heroism. Pyraelists believe that Loper, the devil of their theology, is responsible for much of the world's conflicts, creating periods of strife and chaos, and that Pyrael will select a hero as their avatar. Avatars of Pyrael are said to be gifted with inordinate fortune so long as they follow the path of Pyrael and use such fortunes for the benefit of others, and are even gifted with premonitions of their own imminent defeats so that they may figure out how to avoid them.

Loperists, however, believe Pyrael to be a hedonistic bully, and that Loper creates lesser evils to keep the attention of Pyrael so that they do not turn their thirst for combat and glory into conquest. According to the word of Loper, if Pyrael is left unquenched with a suitable challenge, they will elect for their avatar to be the villain instead of the hero. The cultists believe the often systematic path design of biometal nests to not be simply something adventurers have learned to navigate naturally, but were arranged as such purposefully by Loper to keep Pyrael busy.

## Jamekism

A primarily [naga](nagas.md) religion. The head deity is Jameki (pronouced HAH-may-kee), a god of war and tricks who ousted the other gods responsible for creating Kyroda, cementing his chosen people, the nagas, as the dominant species. Jamekism decrees that no naga may be owned, but that all others may be owned by nagas, due to their holy dominion, and commands that nagas exercise their dominance over others.

The scriptures are full of stories of conquest and cunning naga heroes overcoming those who stand in the way of naga superiority, presenting early naga kind as an underdog being taken advantage of by others to justify the devine reward of rulership.

## Aurorianism

A [hidegvin](hidegvins.md) belief. It lacks a head deity. Instead, it focuses on the aurora borealis, and teaches that the aurora is a devine spiritual realm crossing over with Kyroda. If one were to fly through it and receive the blessing of the grand spirits, they may pass into the spirit world and be granted secret [magics](magic.md).

This religion focuses more on spirituality than worship of any being, teaching the importance of seeking enlightenment so that one may pass through the aurora and be blessed. Hidegvins who follow this faith will gather on the summit of polar mountains in hopes of touching the aurora.

## Ratatism

The [kholu](kholu.md) religion. According to kholu lore, Ratatoskr visited Kyroda millenia ago after being chased off the tree Yggdrasil due to a "difference of opinion" between Tosk and Veorfolnir. Lost and injured, he was taken in by the ancient kholu and shown hospitality despite it being the dead of winter. Impressed by their kindness despite their adversity, Tosk gave them a blessing, shaping their innate magic to align it with the earth element to ensure the ground would always be fertile. When they asked how they could repay him, he said he needed nothing, but if they insisted, they could pay it foward.

Ratatism doesn't have any central doctrine, but the closest thing to it would be "help others, but don't impose, and don't lose your sense of humor". Ratatoskr's role as a messenger god has also influenced the kholu to value sharing information and communication.
